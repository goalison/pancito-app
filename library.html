The Glossary

De-coding the language of the sourdough alchemist. Search for techniques, terms, and tools.

search
TECHNIQUE

Autolyse

A rest period where flour and water are mixed before adding salt and starter. Encourages enzymatic activity and gluten development.

SCIENCE

Hooch

The liquid layer that forms on top of an unfed starter. High in alcohol — it means your starter is hungry and needs feeding.

CRAFT

Banneton

A proofing basket, often rattan or linen-lined, that holds shaped dough and imprints a beautiful spiral pattern on the crust.

TEXTURE

Open Crumb

The aesthetic goal of many bakers: a light, airy internal structure with large, irregular air pockets throughout the loaf.

TECHNIQUE

Stretch & Fold

Gentle dough strengthening during bulk fermentation. Stretch the dough up and fold over itself, rotating the bowl each time — no kneading needed.

PROCESS

Bulk Fermentation

The first rise after mixing. Wild yeast and bacteria ferment the dough at room temperature, building flavor and structure over several hours.

BAKING

Lame

A razor-sharp blade on a handle used to score bread before baking. The score controls where the loaf opens during oven spring.

SCIENCE

Oven Spring

The rapid rise that happens in the first 10–15 minutes of baking as trapped gases expand. A dramatic spring means well-fermented dough.

STARTER

Levain

A pre-ferment built from your starter that is added to the dough. Using a levain gives more control over fermentation rate and flavor profile.

SHAPING

Boule & Bâtard

The two classic sourdough shapes. A boule is round; a bâtard is oval. Each requires a different shaping technique and scoring pattern.
