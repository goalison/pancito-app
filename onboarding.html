Do you already have a sourdough starter?

A sourdough starter is a mixture of flour and water that captures wild yeast from the air. If you already have one, great! If not, we'll walk you through creating yours from scratch — it only takes 10 minutes of hands-on time.

Creating Your Starter

Alison's starter recipe

This process takes 7–10 days. You'll spend about 5 minutes per day. The app will remind you each day.

1

Day 1 — Mix & Rest

~5 minutes hands-on

grain 50g all-purpose flour
eco 50g whole wheat flour
water_drop 100g water (room temperature)

Mix everything in a clean jar until no dry flour remains. Cover loosely with a cloth or loosely-placed lid. Leave at room temperature (68–78°F). That's it for today.

2

Day 2 — Leave it alone

No action required

Don't feed it. Don't stir it. The wild yeast is just beginning to wake up. You might not see much activity yet — that's completely normal.

3+

Days 3–10 — Daily feeding

Repeat every day until ready

remove Discard half (pour out ~100g)
grain Add 100g all-purpose flour
water_drop Add 100g water

Stir well, cover loosely, leave at room temperature. Your starter is ready when it reliably doubles within 4–6 hours of feeding, smells pleasantly sour (like yogurt), and is full of bubbles throughout. This usually happens between Day 7–10 depending on your kitchen temperature.

Tip: Warmer kitchens (75°F+) move faster. Cooler kitchens (65°F) take longer. Both produce great starters — patience is the only ingredient you can't measure.

The Golden Rule

Every 10°F warmer roughly halves your bulk time. Track your dough temperature (not room temp!) with a probe thermometer. 74–78°F is the sweet spot for predictable, flavorful fermentation.

The bench rest (20–30 min) lets gluten relax after pre-shaping. This makes final shaping much smoother — the dough will stretch without tearing and hold its shape beautifully.

Baker's Insight

Cold proofing 8–16 hours develops complex flavor through slow fermentation. The cold also firms up the dough, making it easier to score a beautiful pattern before baking.

Baker's Insight

The Dutch oven creates steam in the first 20 minutes — keeping the crust soft so the loaf expands fully (oven spring). Removing the lid after 20 min lets the Maillard reaction work its magic: that gorgeous golden crunch.
