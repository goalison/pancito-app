What It Is

A flat, rectangular tool, usually made of stainless steel with a comfortable handle, or of flexible plastic. Despite its simplicity, the bench scraper serves multiple critical purposes throughout the baking process — from shaping to cleanup.

Think of it as an extension of your hands. It lets you interact with sticky, high-hydration dough without adding extra flour that would tighten the crumb.

Why It Matters

Managing sticky sourdough dough is one of the biggest challenges for new bakers. The bench scraper lets you work confidently with wet dough — dividing, folding, and shaping — without over-flouring the surface, which would tighten the gluten and close the crumb.

It also makes cleanup effortless, scraping dried flour and sticky bits off your work surface in seconds rather than soaking and scrubbing.

How to Use It

  1. During preshaping, slide the scraper under the dough and use it to pull the dough toward you, building surface tension without sticking your hands in.
  2. To divide dough, push the scraper straight down with a confident, clean cut — avoid sawing motions that tear the gluten.
  3. Use it to transfer shaped dough into your banneton cleanly.
  4. After shaping, use it to scrape flour, dough remnants, and debris off the bench in one smooth pass.
  5. A plastic bowl scraper is ideal for getting every bit of dough out of your mixing bowl.

Tips & Tricks

  • A light flour dusting combined with a slightly damp scraper is the secret to managing sticky dough.
  • Metal scrapers are best for shaping and dividing; plastic bowl scrapers work best for cleaning bowls.
  • Practice confident, small strokes during preshaping — hesitation leads to sticking.
  • Chill the scraper in the fridge briefly when working with very soft, warm dough.
  • Two scrapers at once (one in each hand) is a pro technique for managing batards.

Care Instructions

  • Metal versions: rinse immediately after use and dry thoroughly to prevent rust on the handle rivets.
  • Plastic versions: dishwasher-safe with no concerns.
  • Both types are extremely durable with basic care and will last years.
  • Avoid soaking metal scrapers for extended periods.
