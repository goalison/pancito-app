What It Is

A small handle that holds a razor-sharp, curved or straight blade, used specifically for scoring bread dough just before it goes into the oven. It creates both decorative patterns and functional cuts that control how the loaf opens during baking.

The word "lame" (pronounced "lahm") comes from the French for blade. It's the baker's equivalent of a scalpel — precise, purposeful, and essential.

Why It Matters

During oven spring, the dough expands rapidly as gases heat up inside. Without a score, the loaf will burst wherever the crust is weakest — creating random, uncontrolled tears. Scoring with a lame provides deliberate expansion points, giving the loaf a beautiful, controlled opening.

It also develops the prized "ear" — that crispy, raised edge bakers admire. A sharp blade held at the right angle makes all the difference between a rustic split and an elegant ear.

How to Use It

  1. Score just before oven insertion — never ahead of time, as the dough relaxes and the cut closes.
  2. Hold the blade at a 30–45 degree angle to the surface of the dough for a proper ear.
  3. Make one confident, swift slash — hesitation drags the blade and deflates the dough.
  4. For decorative scoring, work quickly with light strokes before the dough warms up.
  5. Straight blades suit simple slashes; curved blades are ideal for ear-style scoring.

Tips & Tricks

  • Start with a single long slash — it's the most reliable and forgiving score.
  • Chill your dough in the fridge for 30–60 minutes before scoring for a firmer surface.
  • Dust the surface lightly with rice flour so the blade glides without dragging.
  • A dull blade is worse than no blade. Replace it regularly.
  • Wet the blade lightly with water to further reduce sticking on high-hydration doughs.

Care Instructions

  • Handle with extreme caution — the blade is razor-sharp.
  • Rinse the handle and blade immediately after use; dry thoroughly to prevent rust.
  • Store safely away from children and loose items in drawers.
  • Replace blades every few weeks with regular use, or when they feel like they're dragging.
