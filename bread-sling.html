What It Is

A flexible, heat-resistant mat or sheet — usually made from silicone-coated fabric — designed to simplify the most nerve-wracking moment in sourdough baking: transferring your cold, proofed dough into a screaming-hot Dutch oven.

It acts as a hammock for your loaf, letting you lower the dough gently and safely without the traditional acrobatics of flipping a hot lid or fumbling with parchment paper.

Why It Matters

The transfer from banneton to Dutch oven is where many loaves get damaged. Cold, delicate dough can deflate if handled roughly, and the pot is dangerously hot. Traditional methods involve juggling a hot lid while somehow flipping the dough in one motion — it's awkward, risky, and rattles even experienced bakers.

The bread sling eliminates all of that. You score on the sling, grip both sides, and lower the dough in smoothly. It's the safety net your baking routine needs.

How to Use It

  1. Place the sling flat on your work surface before flipping the banneton.
  2. Flip your proofed dough from the banneton directly onto the center of the sling.
  3. Score the dough while it rests on the sling — no rushing required.
  4. Grip both edges of the sling firmly and lower the dough into the preheated Dutch oven in a controlled motion.
  5. Remove the sling after approximately 20 minutes so the loaf bottom can brown directly on the pot.

Tips & Tricks

  • Center the dough on the sling so you have even grip on both sides.
  • Pleat or fold the corners slightly for round Dutch ovens to avoid bunching.
  • Always use quality oven mitts — the pot and sling edges can be very hot.
  • Score confidently and quickly; the cold dough warms up fast once out of the fridge.
  • The sling can be reused dozens of times with proper care.

Care Instructions

  • Wipe clean after each use with a damp cloth and allow to dry completely.
  • Most silicone slings are dishwasher-safe — check your specific product.
  • Avoid sharp objects like the lame blade when the dough is resting on it.
  • Store flat or loosely rolled — do not fold sharply, which can crease the silicone coating.
